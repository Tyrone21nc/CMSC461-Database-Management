Relavant Entities for the Parking Database (DB):
1. Car
    - Entity details
        - car_id (PK = Primary Key, value to identify a specific instance of the car entity; this value can never change hence always returning the same output)
        - make
        - model
        - owner name
        - color
        - license plate
        - car body (sedan, pick-up truck, motocycle, suv, etc.)
        - date added (date car was added to DB)
    - Relation to User:
        - one and only one
    - Relation to Parking Spot:
        - zero or many
2. Parking Spot
    - Entity details
        - parking area name (like parking lot A, B, ...)
        - parking #
        - parking size (certain sizes won't allow certain vehicles of certain bodies to fit in)
        - is_active
    - Relation to Car
        -  zero many (parking spot can have zero or many cars)
3. User
    - Entity details
        - user type (can be student, staff, and visitor)
        - first name
        - last name
        - car amount
        - car(s) plate # (can be a collection, if has many cars in the system)
        - has_parked_car (if true, it means it cannot have another parked car)
        - has_fine(s)
    - Relation to Car
        - zero or more
    - Relation to Payment transaction:
        - zero or more
    - Relation to Notification messaging
        - zero or more
4. Payment transaction
    - Entity details
        - owner of payment
        - payment_id
        - payment amount
        - payment issue date
        - payment due date
        - is_fine_notified
    - Relation to Notification messaging
        - zero or many
    - Relation to User
        - zero or many
5. Notification messaging
    - Entity details
        - name_notification_sent_to (who it was sent to)
        - notification_id
        - notification sent date
    - Relation to Payment transaction
        - zero or many
    - Relation to User
        - zero or many
